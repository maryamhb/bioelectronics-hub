Maryam Habibollahi

Research Fellow in Integrated Bioelectronics, University College London

I design and develop advanced bioelectronic devices and integrated circuits for clinical applications. My research bridges engineering innovation and healthcare challenges, focusing on miniaturised, low-power systems that sense, process, and stimulate biological signals with unprecedented precision.

My work spans the complete bioelectronics pipeline: from mixed-signal CMOS circuit design through clinical translation. I am passionate about closed-loop neuromodulation systems, wearable biomedical devices, and mentoring the next generation of bioelectronics engineers.

Looking forward, I aspire to establish a centre for neurorehabilitation research at UCL, bringing together engineers, clinicians, and neuroscientists to accelerate translation of bioelectronic solutions across neurodegenerative disease diagnostics, monitoring, and therapeutics.
